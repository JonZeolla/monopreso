What is DevSecOps?

A refinement on DevOps. DevOps done well does not need DevSecOps. The focus/refinement is generally useful when seeking our services or information.

Horses vs Unicorns

  • Unicorns are start-ups.
  • In The Phoenix Project, Gene Kim talks about Horses.
  • Horses are Enterprises which adopt DevOps principles, similar to but unlike Unicorns.

Culture: Solve problems in a collaborative way. Focus on the best result.
Automation: Automate-first. Reduce/remove papercuts. Build reusable components.
Lean: Agile practices such as Kanban and Scrum. Don't prematurely solve problems or over-engineer. Limited bureaucracy.
Measurement: Data-driven decision making.
Sharing: Transprency. Share ideas, information, and (most importantly) problems. Not complaining.

Culture

  • Failures will always happen. Do not minimize failures, minimize the impact and recovery time.
  • Empower teams to take action; limit bureaucracy.
  • Focus on collaboratively solving problems. Security as a part of the delivery team.

Automation

  • Process documentation as an automation design document
  • Artifact creation is evidence collection
  • Version control as change mangement
  • Logging and metrics inform decision making (CMMI 4+)
  • Consistency at scale
  • Pre-solve for future urgent requests
  • Requires significant up-front investment to do it right (design, tests, etc.)

Lean

  • Agile: Scrum or Kanban
  • Manage waste (unplanned work), bottlenecks, and delays.
  • Prioritize Just-In-Time (JIT) work in small chunks.

Measurement

  • Change frequency (grease the skids)
  • Mean Time To Recover
  • Lead time / Cycle time
  • Dashboards!!!!!!!!!!!

Sharing

  • Transparency across the organization (and more?)
  • Kerckhoffs's principle
  • Direct sharing to reduce lossy hand-offs (Priorities/backlog)
  • Continuously improve the quality of information, allowing self-service.
Kerckhoffs's principle: a cryptosystem should be secure, even if everything about the system, except the key, is public knowledge.
