Day 2 problems

"A foolish consistency is the hobgoblin of little minds" - Ralph Waldo Emerson Emerson was a philosopher. Standardization isn't always the answer, and we need to be open to rethinking things Let's look at 10 common problems with policies

Spaghetti Governance

Needing to reference 4 different documents to get your answer

Exceptions

Too painful vs too easy. Who can approve exceptions? How approvals/exceptions are submit, tracked, managed. “Unless approved by senior management” - is claiming that it was verbally approved OK?

Mandating the impossible

Saying that employees must have at least WPA2 encryption on their home wifi
This extensive and somewhat overwhelming array of words, phrases, and various linguistic elements, when amalgamated in a manner such as this, ultimately leads, or more accurately meanders, toward a destination, or to be more precise, an endpoint that, paradoxically, seems to be characterized by a notable lack of substantive content or meaningful substance, which is, in itself, a concept worthy of some contemplation or, at the very least, a brief moment of reflection.
Length - way too long and extra information not needed.

Saying so much that it says nothing

Nobody knows why

Why is it worded that way? Does it need to be that specific word? Can we change that word? When can we remove that word?

Broken references

Having internal references to files that get renamed over time, causing auditability to be confusing. Renaming “rules” to “questionnaire” but not updating the reference to it in a Policy, etc.

Overly ambitious

Should/May vs Must. SLO vs SLA. “All” statements vs compliance percentages.

Is it covering what it should?

Too specific or not detailed enough

Policies needing revision for algorithm updates or software versions Actually being usable - what does it take to be compliant with a policy? How do I make this work compliant?

Arbitrary timeframes

90 days, 30 days, continuously, etc.
